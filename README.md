# Conceptual Governance‑Layer Substrate for Metadata‑Only Environments

## Overview
Encrypted and privacy‑preserving messaging environments increasingly serve as commercial surfaces. These environments operate without access to message content or identity signals and rely entirely on metadata‑level interaction events. Such environments require a conceptual governance‑layer substrate capable of transforming interaction metadata into abstract states, organizing those states into cycles and histories, forming conceptual groups, and applying fairness‑based rotation to determine placement.

This repository publishes that substrate at a non‑enabling conceptual level.

- **Group** — a conceptual clustering of entities based on histories.
- **Rotation** — a conceptual fairness mechanism applied within groups.
- **Placement** — a conceptual ordering derived from group and history.
